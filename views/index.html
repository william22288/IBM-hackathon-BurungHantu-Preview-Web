What Is Accounting?

Accounting is the process of recording financial transactions pertaining to a business. The accounting process includes summarizing, analyzing, and reporting these transactions to oversight agencies, regulators, and tax collection entities. The financial statements used in accounting are a concise summary of financial transactions over an accounting period, summarizing a company's operations, financial position, and cash flows.

Key Takeaways

  • Accounting is essential for decision-making, cost planning, and measuring economic performance.
  • Bookkeepers can handle basic accounting, but CPAs are required for more advanced tasks.
  • Types of accounting include managerial accounting, cost accounting, and tax accounting.
  • GAAP is used in the U.S. for preparing financial statements, while IFRS is used in many other countries.

Understanding Accounting

Accounting is a key function for businesses of any size. It may be handled by bookkeepers or accountants at smaller firms and by large finance departments in bigger companies. The reports generated are essential for management decision-making and operational insights.

History of Accounting

Accounting dates back to ancient times, with early practices in Mesopotamia, Egypt, and Babylon. Luca Pacioli, known as the "Father of Accounting," published the first book on the double-entry bookkeeping system in 1494. The modern accounting profession was solidified in the 19th century with the formation of the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales.

Types of Accounting

  • Financial Accounting: Produces interim and annual financial statements and is often audited by external firms.
  • Managerial Accounting: Generates reports for internal use by management to make strategic business decisions.
  • Cost Accounting: Helps businesses determine product pricing by analyzing production costs.
  • Tax Accounting: Ensures compliance with tax rules and strategizes to minimize tax liability.

Accounting Careers

Careers in accounting vary and include roles such as auditor, forensic accountant, tax accountant, managerial accountant, IT accountant, and controller. The Big Four (Ernst & Young, KPMG, PwC, Deloitte) dominate the industry, providing auditing, consulting, and advisory services.

Accounting Standards

GAAP and IFRS are the primary standards used globally for preparing financial statements, ensuring consistency and comparability across industries. Tax accountants adhere to guidance from bodies like the IRS.

Major Accounting Software

Common software for small businesses includes QuickBooks and Xero, while larger enterprises may use Oracle or Sage solutions.

The Accounting Cycle

The accounting cycle involves steps such as collecting transaction information, posting journal entries, and preparing financial statements, culminating in accurate financial reporting.

Cash Method vs. Accrual Method

The accrual method records transactions when they occur, while the cash method records them only when cash is exchanged. Public companies must use the accrual method.

Importance of Accounting

Accounting supports strategic planning, financial forecasting, compliance, and growth. It ensures that businesses can make informed decisions, secure funding, and maintain financial health.
